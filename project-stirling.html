The Project

Full CAD Recreation of a Thermodynamic Engine

The Stirling engine was selected because it contains a wide variety of mechanical sub-systems — pistons, displacers, crankshafts, connecting rods, flywheel, bearings, and a burner assembly — requiring a different modelling approach for each and tight dimensional control across all mating interfaces.

Each of the 39 items in the BOM was modelled individually before being brought into a top-level assembly, forcing attention to fit, clearance, and interference conditions across the full mechanism. The work was then documented to formal engineering drawing standards with a title block, tolerance stack, and drawing number series.

Machining

Shop Floor Execution

Multiple components were physically machined — not just modelled — putting the CAD work into real production context:

  • Lathe: Turned cylindrical features (shafts, pistons, cylinders) to precise diameters with surface finish control and tight roundness requirements
  • CNC: Programmed and ran toolpaths for 2D profiles, pockets, and drilled patterns
  • Milling machine: Faced stock, cut slots, and produced flat reference surfaces to drawing dimension

What This Demonstrates

Skills Employers Care About

Formal drawing standards: Producing a 3-sheet drawing package with title block, revision, drawing numbers (SE-001/002/003), and explicit tolerance callouts is what employers in aerospace and energy expect from hired engineers — not just screenshots of a model.

DFM awareness: Machining parts after modelling them closes the loop — you learn quickly where CAD assumptions fail in contact with real toolpaths and material behavior.

Multi-machine proficiency: Lathe, CNC, and mill are the core triad of a precision machine shop — comfort across all three is expected in aerospace and energy manufacturing roles.

Results & Takeaways

What I Learned

Modelling a complete mechanism teaches you to think in assemblies from the start. Every geometric decision on Part 1 affects how Part 22 fits, and cascading errors are expensive to fix late. The drawing package formalized this — writing tolerances forces you to decide exactly what matters dimensionally and what doesn’t.

Time on the machines built respect for tolerances that CAD makes look easy. A 0.003” fit demands real attention to setup, tool selection, and cutting parameters — not just a number on a drawing.

Relevant To

Any role requiring engineered-to-drawing mechanical components — aerospace structures, turbomachinery, energy system hardware, or precision instrumentation — directly benefits from the CAD-to-shop-floor experience and formal drawing discipline this project developed.

The formal drawing package (title block, revision, tolerance callouts, numbered BOM) matches industry documentation standards expected in aerospace and defense engineering environments.
