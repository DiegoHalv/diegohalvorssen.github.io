The Problem

A Physical Problem Deserved a Physical Solution

Phone notifications for rain are easy to miss when you are rushing out the door. The goal was an ambient, physical alert — something that exists in your line of sight at the exit and cannot be swiped away.

Beyond the product idea, this was a deliberate exercise in the full mechatronics loop: external software data → microcontroller logic → physical actuation → custom mechanical enclosure.

System Architecture

How It Connects

🌐 Weather API → Live rain forecast data
⚡ Elegoo UNO R3 (Arduino) → Parses data, triggers logic
🔧 Servo Actuator → Raises physical flag via PWM
🖨 3D-Printed Enclosure → Custom-fit housing (Fusion 360)

Engineering Decisions

Where Judgment Called the Shots

Physical vs. digital alert: A mechanical flag in your line of sight as you leave is harder to ignore than an LED or app notification.

Custom enclosure: Designed the housing in Fusion 360 specifically for the electronics layout — clean cable routing, correct port placement, and a form factor sized to sit near a door.

Polling interval: Balanced update frequency against power consumption and API rate limits by polling on a fixed timed interval rather than continuously.

Results & Takeaways

Outcome

Delivered a fully functional, user-ready prototype that closes the loop from live software data to physical world actuation inside a professionally designed enclosure.

This project showcases the mechatronics skill. Wrapping custom-designed mechanical enclosures around functional electronics and external software APIs.

What I Learned

The hardest part was designing the enclosure around the electronics — physical constraints (port locations, wire routing, assembly order) must be accounted for from the start, not solved after.

Iterating in Fusion 360 before printing saved significant time and material — a habit that transfers directly to production manufacturing.
